# ALKYNES -- Short & Structured Notes

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 1️⃣ Definition of Alkynes

-   Alkynes are **unsaturated hydrocarbons**.
-   They contain at least one **triple bond (C≡C)**.
-   General formula:

CₙH₂ₙ₋₂

**Reason:**\
Alkynes have two hydrogen atoms less than alkenes and four less than
alkanes.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### First Member

-   **Ethyne (C₂H₂)**
-   Common name: **Acetylene**

**Used in:** - Oxy-acetylene welding (very high temperature flame)

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 2️⃣ Structure of Triple Bond

**Example:** Ethyne (HC≡CH)

Each carbon atom is: - sp hybridised - Linear structure - Bond angle =
180°

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Bond Formation

Triple bond consists of: - 1 Sigma (σ) bond - 2 Pi (π) bonds

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### Important Bond Data

  Bond   Bond Length   Bond Enthalpy
  ------ ------------- ---------------
  C≡C    120 pm        823 kJ/mol
  C=C    134 pm        681 kJ/mol
  C--C   154 pm        348 kJ/mol

👉 Triple bond is shortest and strongest.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 3️⃣ Nomenclature of Alkynes (IUPAC)

1.  Choose longest chain containing triple bond.
2.  Number from nearest end.
3.  Replace "-ane" with "-yne".

### Examples

-   HC≡CH → Ethyne\
-   CH₃--C≡CH → Propyne\
-   CH₃--C≡C--CH₃ → But-2-yne\
-   HC≡C--CH₂--CH₃ → But-1-yne

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 4️⃣ Isomerism in Alkynes

Alkynes show: - Position isomerism - Chain isomerism

### Example (C₅H₈)

-   Pent-1-yne\
-   Pent-2-yne\
-   3-Methylbut-1-yne

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# PREPARATION OF ALKYNES

Alkynes are mainly prepared by elimination reactions (removal
reactions).\
To make a triple bond, atoms are removed from neighbouring carbons.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 🔹 1. From Calcium Carbide (Industrial Method)

### Step 1: Preparation of Calcium Carbide

CaO + 3C → CaC₂ + CO

(Lime is heated with coke.)

### Step 2: Reaction with Water

CaC₂ + 2H₂O → Ca(OH)₂ + C₂H₂

Calcium carbide reacts with water to give **ethyne gas**.

**Importance:** - Cheap method - Used in welding - Produces high
temperature flame

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 🔹 2. From Vicinal Dihalides (Important Method)

Vicinal dihalides: Two halogens attached to adjacent carbons.

Example: CH₂Br--CH₂Br

### Step 1: First Elimination (Alcoholic KOH)

CH₂Br--CH₂Br → CH₂=CHBr

One H and one Br removed → forms alkenyl halide.

### Step 2: Second Elimination (NaNH₂)

CH₂=CHBr → HC≡CH

Another HBr removed → triple bond formed.

**Why strong base?**\
Second hydrogen removal is difficult, so NaNH₂ is required.

**Important Concept:**\
Two eliminations → Double bond → Triple bond

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 🌿 PHYSICAL PROPERTIES OF ALKYNES

Physical properties depend on: - Molecular mass - Intermolecular forces

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 🔹 1. Physical State

-   First 3 alkynes → Gases
-   Next few → Liquids
-   Higher ones → Solids

As carbon chain increases: - Molecular weight increases - Van der Waals
forces increase - Boiling point increases

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 🔹 2. Colour & Odour

-   All alkynes are colourless.
-   Ethyne has slight smell.
-   Others mostly odourless.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 🔹 3. Solubility

In Water: - Insoluble

Reason: Alkynes are non-polar, water is polar.

In Organic Solvents: - Soluble in ether, benzene, carbon tetrachloride.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 🔹 4. Density

-   Alkynes are lighter than water.
-   Float on water.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## 🔹 5. Boiling Point Trend

Boiling point increases with increase in carbon number.

Straight chain → Higher boiling point\
Branched chain → Lower boiling point

Reason: Straight chains pack better.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 🌿 CHEMICAL PROPERTIES OF ALKYNES

Alkynes are reactive because: - They contain a triple bond. - Triple
bond has 2 π bonds. - π bonds are weak and easily attacked.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## A️⃣ Acidic Nature of Alkynes

Only terminal alkynes (HC≡C--H) show acidity.

### Why?

-   Carbon is sp hybridised.
-   50% s-character.
-   Hydrogen can leave easily as H⁺.

### Reaction with Sodium

HC≡CH + Na → HC≡CNa + ½H₂

Hydrogen gas is released.

### Reaction with NaNH₂

HC≡CH + NaNH₂ → HC≡CNa + NH₃

Only terminal alkynes show this reaction.

### Acidity Order

Alkyne \> Alkene \> Alkane\
(sp \> sp² \> sp³)

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## B️⃣ Addition Reactions of Alkynes

Triple bond can add two molecules.

### 🔹 1. Addition of Hydrogen

HC≡CH → CH₂=CH₂ → CH₃--CH₃

Special case: Lindlar catalyst stops at alkene stage.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### 🔹 2. Addition of Halogens

Step 1: Alkyne → Dihaloalkene\
Step 2: → Tetrahaloalkane

Test: Bromine solution loses brown colour.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### 🔹 3. Addition of HX (HCl, HBr, HI)

Two molecules add following Markovnikov rule.

HC≡CH + HBr\
→ CH₂=CHBr\
→ CH₃--CHBr₂

Product: Gem-dihalide

------------------------------------------------------------------------

### 🔹 4. Addition of Water (Hydration)

Reagent: HgSO₄ + Dil. H₂SO₄

First product: Enol (unstable)\
Enol → Converts to aldehyde or ketone (Tautomerism)

Examples: - Ethyne → Ethanal\
- Propyne → Propanone

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## C️⃣ Polymerisation of Alkynes

### 🔹 1. Linear Polymerisation

n(HC≡CH) → Polyacetylene

Conducts electricity under special conditions.

### 🔹 2. Cyclic Polymerisation

3 HC≡CH\
(873 K, red hot iron tube)\
→ Benzene

Important reaction: Aliphatic → Aromatic conversion.
